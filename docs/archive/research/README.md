# Archived research documents

Documents in this directory were drafted as active PRSM research
artifacts but have been re-scoped as **deferred research exploration**
rather than active protocol standards.

They are preserved here (not deleted) because the research content
may be revived later through the §9 standards-ratification process
if the relevant circumstances change.

## 2026-04-24: PRSM-CIS-1 standard + derivative economic model

- `2026-04-19-confidential-inference-silicon-standard.md` — early
  draft of the confidential-silicon standard.
- `2026-04-21-prsm-cis-1-confidential-inference-silicon.md` — full
  PRSM-CIS-1 spec.
- `2026-04-21-prsm-economic-model-cis-silicon.md` (renamed from
  `2026-04-21-prsm-economic-model-white-paper.md`) — **PRSM-ECON-WP-1**
  economic model paper. Prices Prismatica enterprise value bottom-up
  from CIS chip design costs, per-chip-year revenue under Phase 3
  marketplace pricing, meganode unit economics, and a two-revenue-
  stream framing (commercial services + FTNS treasury). Archived
  alongside CIS-1 because: (a) the paper's core thesis is CIS-chip-
  priced; (b) the two-stream framing is superseded by Prismatica's
  actual nine-stream architecture per `Prismatica_Vision.md` §2-3;
  and (c) v1 bull/base/bear headline numbers are anchored to CIS-C1/
  C2/C3 adoption assumptions that no longer apply. A new economic
  model paper priced around Prismatica's six-stream core business
  should be drafted if and when the Foundation wants a successor
  artifact.

**Why archived:** Per the canonical Vision docs in the founder's
Obsidian vault (`PRSM_Vision.md`, `Prismatica_Vision.md`,
`PRSM_Tokenomics.md`), PRSM's launch-day scope does not include
chip design / fabrication / silicon certification. Prismatica's
day-1 business is six commercial revenue streams (commons curation,
T3/T4 compute operations, FTNS treasury, protocol-native VC fund,
commissioned datasets, domain foundation models) — none of which
involves Prismatica operating as a silicon first-implementer.

The Foundation's certification role is correspondingly rescoped: the
Foundation currently ratifies no silicon standards. Active standards
are PRSM-SUPPLY-1 (supply-side diversity), PRSM-POLICY-JURISDICTION-1
(Foundation boundary subsidiary policy), and protocol-ATTEST-n
(attestation protocols for TEE work, Phase 2 Rings 7-10).

**What this means for investors, counsel, and auditors:** CIS-1 is
not part of the Foundation's day-1 standards portfolio, is not a
line item in Prismatica's use-of-proceeds, and is not an active
engineering track. If later revived as an active research arc, it
would re-enter via the §9 ratification path documented in
`docs/2026-04-21-prsm-gov-1-foundation-governance-charter.md`.
