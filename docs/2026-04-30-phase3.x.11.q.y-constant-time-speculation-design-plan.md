# Phase 3.x.11.q.y — Constant-Time Speculation (Tier C + Speculation Composition)

**Status:** Design draft (not yet started).
**Closes:** the named "speculation + Tier C structurally denied" deferral that has been carrying since Phase 3.x.11.y. Threat-model addendum §3.6.1-3.6.3 + §3.7 cite Phase 3.x.11.q.y as the closure milestone for three new content-correlated wire surfaces that Phase 3.x.11.q's chain-level decorators DON'T cover (accept-rate channel, `proposed_token_probs` per VERIFY round, adaptive K cross-round correlation).

## 1. Problem statement

Phase 3.x.11.q lifts the Tier C structural deny **for non-speculative sharded decode** via chain-level decorators. Speculation continues to be denied because three new surfaces sit ABOVE the StreamToken iterator that the chain-level decorators wrap:

1. **`RunLayerSliceResponse.accepted_count` on the wire (per VERIFY round).** Each verifying response ships an integer in `[0, K]`. A stage operator OR a network observer with TLS termination on inter-stage links learns the per-round acceptance count. Even if the executor's StreamToken iterator emits at constant cadence (M1) or as one trailing frame (M2), the per-stage wire still carries the integer.

2. **`RunLayerSliceRequest.proposed_token_probs` on the wire (K floats per VERIFY round).** Each verifying request ships K floats — the draft's per-token confidence at d_1..d_K. Strongest leak in v1: for prompts where the draft is highly confident (low-entropy q), this approaches a near-bit-for-bit leak of WHICH tokens the draft proposed.

3. **Adaptive K cross-round correlation.** The executor's rolling-window adaptive K (halve <25% / double >75%) means the K-value used in subsequent rounds depends on previous rounds' accept-rates — which depend on prompt content. An observer who can correlate `proposed_token_ids` length across consecutive VERIFY requests learns coarse-grained "this request kept getting low accept-rate" signal.

The chain-level decorators in Phase 3.x.11.q gate the executor → caller iterator; the per-stage wire (executor → stage RPCs) continues to carry these three surfaces unchanged.

## 2. Scope

In scope:

1. **Encrypted/padded `proposed_token_probs` wire field.** Two design candidates explored in §3.1:
   - **Padded wire only.** All K floats replaced with a constant zero-padded vector to a fixed K_max length; tail's rejection-sampling helper is fed the padded array. Loses the algorithmic correctness of the §2.2 marginal-equals-target invariant (Option C.1 already drifts under stochastic q; padding to zeros further drifts).
   - **Encrypted wire** (recommended). Probs are AES-GCM-encrypted under a per-request shared secret negotiated between executor and tail at PREFILL time; the per-stage transport sees opaque ciphertext only. Tail decrypts before passing to the rejection helper. Mathematically the same Option C.1 algorithm; the wire surface becomes content-uncorrelated.

2. **Masked `accepted_count` wire field.** Two design candidates:
   - **Constant-K commitment.** The tail commits in advance to ALWAYS returning `accepted_count = K` (i.e., always emit the K+1th bonus regardless of actual rejection). Sampling correctness preserved by adjusting the rejection helper to discard rejected drafts AFTER the wire commitment. Wire becomes constant-byte.
   - **Encrypted accepted_count** (similar pattern to probs; recommended for consistency).

3. **Flat-K mode.** Operator opt-in to disable adaptive K under Tier C (`speculation_depth` stays constant across all VERIFY rounds). Cross-round correlation eliminated; perf cost is the lost adaptive perf gain (10-30% on hard prompts).

4. **Composition with Phase 3.x.11.q decorators.** The constant-time speculative path uses ONE chain-level decorator (M1 or M2) on the executor → caller iterator AND the encrypted-wire + flat-K mitigations on the per-stage executor → stage path. The decorator is unchanged; the per-stage encryption is new.

5. **Routing-layer extension.** `ParallaxScheduledExecutor` gains a `tier_c_speculation_enabled: bool = False` constructor flag. When `True` AND `request.content_tier == ContentTier.C` AND `request.temperature > 0` AND `tier_c_chain_executor` is wired, route to the speculation-capable decorator chain. When `False` (default), Tier C + speculation continues to be denied (no change from sprint-6 behavior).

Out of scope (defer):

- **Per-stage timing masking** for full-network constant-time. Phase 3.x.11.q.x bundle.
- **Mid-stream cadence change** based on observed accept-rate variance.
- **Multi-armed-bandit adaptive K** (the sprint-5 honest-scope deferral). Even when not under Tier C.
- **Padded `proposed_token_probs`** (rejected in favor of encrypted; see §3.1).
- **Accept-rate channel masking via cover traffic** (constant fake VERIFY rounds even on perfect-accept streams). Adds wire bandwidth without proportional security gain; defer.
- **Authenticated rollback envelope** (carried-forward Phase 3.x.11.y.x' deferral). Independent of this slice.

## 3. Design

### 3.1 Encrypted vs padded wire — decision

**Padded wire (rejected).** All K floats replaced with zero-padded vector of length K_max. Loses Option C.1 algorithmic correctness: the rejection helper computes residual `r = max(0, p - q_C1)` where `q_C1(d_i) = q(d_i)`; if `q(d_i)` is replaced with 0, the residual becomes `p` (no rejection happens, every position emits the verifier's argmax). Output distribution drifts catastrophically.

**Encrypted wire (recommended).** Per-request AES-GCM key negotiated at PREFILL time (Diffie-Hellman or pre-shared). Probs are encrypted at the executor before wire send; decrypted at the tail before passing to the rejection helper. Wire surface becomes constant-bytes ciphertext (no content correlation). Algorithmic correctness preserved.

**Decision: encrypted wire.** Wire-format extension: `RunLayerSliceRequest.encrypted_proposed_token_probs: Optional[bytes]`. When set, replaces `proposed_token_probs`. Co-set invariant: NOT both can be set simultaneously (operator chooses one mode at construction).

### 3.2 Constant-K commitment for `accepted_count`

The tail's `apply_lm_head_and_sample_batch_with_rejection` returns `(verified_token_ids, accepted_count)`. Today `len(verified_token_ids) == accepted_count + 1` and the executor's loop emits exactly `accepted_count + 1` tokens then runs rollback for `K - accepted_count` positions. Under Tier C constant-time:

- Tail ALWAYS returns `accepted_count = K` and `verified_token_ids` of length `K + 1`.
- The executor's per-stage wire sees `accepted_count = K` always (constant value; no leak).
- The rejection helper still does the §2.2 logic internally; for rejected drafts at position i < K, the returned `verified_token_ids[i]` is set to the rejection-sampled correction (instead of the proposed `d_i`); the tail emits the K+1 corrections + bonus AS IF all K were accepted.
- Rollback is always `K + 1 - len(emitted)`; under constant-K commitment, rollback is always 0 if the executor emits K+1 (M1: paced over time; M2: joined into one frame). No `RollbackCacheRequest` is ever broadcast under Tier C — wire surface eliminated.

Algorithmic invariant: the marginal output distribution is unchanged (still §2.2 + Option C.1). Only the wire-side `accepted_count` loses its content-correlation.

**Implication for `verified_token_ids`.** Today: `len == accepted_count + 1` (narrowed from K+1 in v1 greedy). Under constant-K commitment: `len == K + 1` always. The runner-side validator at `_sample_tail_verify_stochastic` needs a "constant-K mode" branch that enforces the constant-K invariant.

### 3.3 Flat-K mode

Simple: when `tier_c_speculation_enabled and content_tier == ContentTier.C`, the executor's adaptive-K state machine is bypassed. K stays at `speculation_depth` for all rounds. Already gated to `use_stochastic` per Phase 3.x.11.y.x sprint-5 round-1 review L3 — adding the Tier C check is one extra `and` clause.

### 3.4 Composition with Phase 3.x.11.q chain-level decorators

The chain-level M1/M2 decorator wraps the executor's `execute_chain_streaming` iterator. Today (Phase 3.x.11.q) it's applied in `ParallaxScheduledExecutor.execute_streaming` for non-speculative Tier C. For speculation under Tier C, the same decorator wraps the speculative executor instance. The new code lives at the executor's per-stage RPC layer (encrypted probs + constant-K commitment), NOT at the chain-iterator layer.

```python
# Operator config (production):
chain_executor = RpcChainExecutor(
    ..., draft_model=hf_draft, speculation_depth=4,
    encrypted_probs_mode=True,  # NEW kwarg in 3.x.11.q.y
    constant_k_commitment=True,  # NEW kwarg in 3.x.11.q.y
)
tier_c_executor = make_tier_c_sharded_executor(
    chain_executor, mode='m1', cadence_seconds=0.5,
)
parallax_executor = ParallaxScheduledExecutor(
    ..., chain_executor=normal_executor,
    tier_c_chain_executor=tier_c_executor,
    tier_c_speculation_enabled=True,
)
```

The factory + decorators DON'T need new args — the speculation-awareness lives at `RpcChainExecutor` construction (the inner of the decorator).

### 3.5 Honest scope

- **Per-stage wire timing still leaks.** Same as Phase 3.x.11.q. Phase 3.x.11.q.x for full-network masking.
- **Encrypted-wire DH key negotiation adds round-trip overhead** at PREFILL. Mitigate by piggybacking the DH ephemeral on the existing `HandoffToken` structure (no new RPC); accept the additional crypto compute.
- **Constant-K commitment loses the perf-by-acceptance-rate optimization.** Under perfect-accept streams (matched draft + verifier), `accepted_count == K` was the natural case; the commitment is free. Under low-accept-rate streams (mismatched draft / hard prompts), the executor was emitting fewer tokens per VERIFY round; the commitment forces it to emit K+1 per round REGARDLESS, so total compute increases (the rejected-draft positions still run through the verifier's rejection-sampling correction, just under wraps). Operator perf budget tradeoff.
- **Cover traffic for accept-rate channel** (constant fake VERIFY rounds on perfect-accept streams) deferred. The 0-rollback-broadcast invariant under constant-K commitment already eliminates the `RollbackCacheRequest.n_positions_to_drop` indirect channel.

## 4. Tasks

### Task 1 — Wire-format extension
**Scope:** `RunLayerSliceRequest.encrypted_proposed_token_probs: Optional[bytes]` field. Co-set with `proposed_token_probs` invariant: at most one of the two can be set on a single request. Signing-payload commitment to the ciphertext bytes (the executor signs the encrypted bytes, not the plaintext — same pattern as today). Omit-when-None canonical encoding preserves byte-equivalence with pre-3.x.11.q.y dispatches.

**Tests:** ≥7 — round-trip + omit-when-default byte-equivalence; co-set-with-plaintext-probs rejection; range/length validation on ciphertext bytes; signing-payload coverage; v1 (no encrypted field) → v2 server backwards-compat.

### Task 2 — Per-request key negotiation
**Scope:** AES-GCM 256-bit key derivation from a Diffie-Hellman ephemeral piggybacked on `HandoffToken`. Key is per-request; tail derives the symmetric key from its identity private key + the request's DH ephemeral. Encrypt + decrypt helpers with explicit AAD = (request_id || stage_index) to bind each ciphertext to its dispatch.

**Tests:** ≥6 — round-trip enc/dec produces identical plaintext; wrong-key tail rejects; AAD mismatch (replayed across stages) rejects; ciphertext is 16-byte-aligned; ciphertext bytes don't correlate with plaintext entropy (basic chi-squared smoke).

### Task 3 — Runner-side constant-K mode
**Scope:** `ShardedAutoregressiveRunner` gains a `constant_k_commitment: bool` constructor flag. When True, `_sample_tail_verify_stochastic` always returns `(verified_ids, K)` regardless of actual `accepted_count`; the rejected-draft positions are filled with the rejection helper's correction tokens IN PLACE of the accepted-prefix tokens beyond the actual acceptance point. Algorithmic invariant: the marginal output distribution is unchanged.

**Tests:** ≥6 — constant-K mode always returns `accepted_count == K`; verified_ids is K+1 entries; marginal output distribution converges to the §2.2 target across 5000 trials (Leviathan-2023 invariant under constant-K); rollback broadcast is suppressed (executor sees 0-positions-to-drop).

### Task 4 — Executor-side encrypted-probs path + flat-K gate
**Scope:** `RpcChainExecutor` gains `encrypted_probs_mode: bool = False` constructor kwarg. When True, the executor encrypts `proposed_token_probs` before dispatch and sets `encrypted_proposed_token_probs` on the wire. `ParallaxScheduledExecutor.execute_streaming` gains the `tier_c_speculation_enabled: bool = False` flag and the routing branch composing all four mitigations (chain-level M1/M2 + encrypted probs + constant-K + flat-K). Adaptive K is bypassed under Tier C even at v2.

**Tests:** ≥6 — encrypted probs path + flat-K under Tier C; verified-ids length K+1 confirmed end-to-end; ciphertext on wire (not plaintext); decryption at runner before rejection helper; routing fallback (Tier C speculation + decorator unwired → structured failure naming the operator-fixable misconfig).

### Task 5 — E2E with mocked observer + real distilgpt2
**Scope:** integration test asserting (a) ciphertext on wire is content-uncorrelated (chi-squared smoke against random bytes); (b) `accepted_count` on wire is constant K across multiple VERIFY rounds; (c) `RollbackCacheRequest` is never broadcast; (d) end-to-end output is statistically equivalent to non-Tier-C speculation (TV < 0.35 vs analytical softmax-T marginal, same as Phase 3.x.11.y.x sprint-5 acceptance bar).

**Tests:** ≥4 — encrypted-wire smoke; constant-K-on-wire pin; zero-rollback pin; statistical-correctness vs sprint-5 baseline.

### Task 6 — Threat-model addendum §3.8 + audit-prep §7.14
**Scope:** Phase 3.x.11.q.y is the load-bearing closure of §3.6.1-3.6.3's three carry-forward surfaces. Add §3.8 documenting (1) what the encrypted-wire + constant-K + flat-K mitigations COVER, (2) what the per-stage timing surface STILL leaks (Phase 3.x.11.q.x), (3) the encryption-key-leak-equivalence honest-scope point (a stage operator with valid identity can decrypt; the encryption defends against passive network observers, not stage operators). Add audit-prep §7.14. Threat-model addendum bumps to 1.4.

## 6. Honest scope (carries forward)

- **Per-stage wire timing still observable.** Phase 3.x.11.q.x bundle (per-stage cadence wrapper for full-network masking).
- **Stage operator with valid identity can decrypt.** The encryption defends against passive network observers, not stage operators with anchor-verified identity. R3 S1 attacker model unchanged.
- **DH key negotiation latency** at PREFILL adds ~5-10ms round-trip. Acceptable trade for the wire-surface elimination.
- **Constant-K perf cost on low-accept-rate streams.** Forces the verifier to compute K+1 corrections per round even when actual acceptance is < K. Operator perf budget tradeoff.
- **Cover traffic for accept-rate channel** (constant fake VERIFY rounds on perfect-accept streams) deferred — the 0-rollback-broadcast invariant under constant-K commitment is the v1 mitigation.
- **Multi-armed-bandit adaptive K** still deferred (independent of Tier C).
- **Authenticated rollback envelope** still deferred (Phase 3.x.11.y.x' carryover; not used under constant-K commitment anyway).
