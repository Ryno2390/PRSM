# PRSM Foundation Officer Job Descriptions + Outreach Pack

**Date:** 2026-04-23
**Owner:** engineering lead (recruitment delegated to Foundation + legal counsel post-formation)
**Status:** Draft ready for legal-counsel review and Q3 2026 board recruitment
**Depends on:** `docs/2026-04-23-foundation-jurisdiction-scoping.md` (Cayman primary) + `docs/2026-04-23-legal-counsel-shortlist-and-rfp.md` (counsel selection)

## 0. What this is

PRSM-GOV-1 §4.4 specifies a 5-7 seat Foundation board across five seat
categories. The pitch deck slide 11 targets the first 2 external board
members by Q3 2026. This doc provides:

1. Per-seat **job descriptions** that a candidate can read and know what
   they'd be signing up for.
2. **Compensation philosophy** — what the Foundation will pay, in what
   form, under what conditions.
3. **Independence + conflicts rubric** — how the Foundation decides
   whether a candidate is eligible.
4. **Candidate-profile matrices** — the concrete shortlist rubric per
   seat, to turn "I know someone who might be good" into a ranked
   evaluation.
5. **Outreach templates** — pre-written pitch emails the founder can
   send to prospective candidates, keyed by seat.
6. **Candidate tracking table** — response-management format that
   mirrors the auditor + legal shortlist docs.

Nothing here binds legal counsel or the Foundation — this is
pre-work for the Q3 2026 recruitment cycle so counsel has concrete
structures to confirm, and the founder has concrete scripts to send.

## 1. Board structure recap (from PRSM-GOV-1 §4.4)

| Seat category | Count | Selection mechanism | Compensation model (proposed) |
|---------------|-------|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| Academic / research | 1-2 | Committee-nominated + 2/3 board confirmation | Annual stipend in FTNS + travel |
| PRSM-staked community | 2 | Token-weighted staker vote (Phase 7+); founder-appointed pre-Phase-7 | Annual stipend in FTNS |
| Independent technical | 1-2 | Direct appointment + 2/3 confirmation | Annual stipend in FTNS + travel |
| Ecosystem operators | 1 | Rotating; 2/3 confirmation | Annual stipend in FTNS |
| Founder (bootstrap only) | 1 | Charter-granted during bootstrap phase | **Uncompensated** (per GOV-1 §8.2) |

**Term:** 3 years, staggered (one-third turn over annually after full
ramp). **Max:** 2 consecutive terms (6 years), then mandatory 1-term
off-board before standing again.

**Target board size at full ramp:** 7 voting seats. **Initial target:**
2 external members by Q3 2026 + founder = 3 seats filled; remaining 4
fill progressively through 2026-2027.

**Hard constraints (GOV-1):**
- Max 1 Prismatica-affiliated member at any time.
- Max 1 member affiliated with any single other implementer.
- Mid-term conflict triggers resignation or 2/3 removal vote.

## 2. Compensation philosophy

### 2.1 Principles

- **Non-profit public-service framing.** Board service is partially a
  public-good contribution. Compensation is a recognition of time cost,
  not an investment vehicle.
- **FTNS-denominated annual stipend** (not USDC) so board members have
  aligned incentives with protocol success without it being an
  investment product. Target magnitude: equivalent to ~$50K/yr USD at
  FTNS-reference price for a regular director seat; Executive Director
  at market non-profit rate.
- **No board equity.** No PRSM equity or options granted. Board members
  who want PRSM exposure buy FTNS on the open market (post-TGE, through
  permitted on-ramps) or stake existing FTNS — the same way any other
  participant does.
- **No revenue-sharing.** Board members do not receive percentage-of-
  protocol-revenue compensation. That pattern has been a governance
  failure mode at comparable foundations — PRSM-GOV-1 §4.3 explicitly
  forbids it.
- **Travel + expense reimbursement at cost.** Standard for 2-in-person
  meetings per year + conference speaking + counsel review of
  Foundation materials.
- **Transparent disclosure** annually per GOV-1 §7 (to be added).
  Board compensation published in every annual report.

### 2.2 Benchmark references for counsel + board finance committee

- Ethereum Foundation board: largely uncompensated historically;
  operational staff at market. Recent public reports suggest board
  members receive reimbursement-only.
- Web3 Foundation: annual stipend in DOT-equivalent, modest (~$40-80K/yr
  range per public disclosure circa 2024).
- Tezos Foundation: board compensation disclosed; similar magnitude.
- US 501(c) nonprofit directors: typically uncompensated except for
  expenses. Cayman Foundation Companies have more flexibility but
  should stay in the non-profit-directors compensation band.

### 2.3 Bootstrap phase caveats

Per GOV-1 §8:
- **Founder's seat is uncompensated** during bootstrap (the founder
  draws Prismatica executive compensation separately).
- Early board stipends may be partially or fully deferred to the
  Foundation's first FTNS treasury allocation — board members
  accepting stipends in deferred form recognize they're taking a
  dependency on the Foundation's operating-revenue ramp.

## 3. Independence + conflicts rubric

Every candidate is evaluated against three independence axes before
nomination + confirmation.

### 3.1 Commercial independence

| Disqualifier | Why |
|--------------|-----|
| Material equity (>1%) or executive role at any PRSM implementer (Prismatica, or any T4 meganode operator / chip vendor) | GOV-1 §4.4 forbids multiple affiliations per implementer + §4.3 forbids Foundation-implementer revenue-sharing |
| Current contract work billed to PRSM or any implementer | Ongoing paid relationship creates fiduciary conflict |
| Board service at any ≥$10B-market-cap publicly-traded AI-compute company | Structural conflict on the inference-neutrality mission |
| Employed by NVIDIA, AMD, Intel, Google Cloud, AWS, Azure at executive or VP level | Same — the verticals PRSM is explicitly neutral against |

### 3.2 Governance independence

| Disqualifier | Why |
|--------------|-----|
| Currently under SEC/CFTC/FINRA enforcement or disciplinary action | Foundation-level compliance risk |
| Named in material litigation against a crypto protocol | Can be public-record research by legal counsel |
| Has resigned "for cause" from another foundation board in past 5 years | Self-disclosure required; background-check by counsel |

### 3.3 Mission alignment (not legally disqualifying, but selection-criteria)

| Signal | Direction |
|--------|-----------|
| Prior public writing supporting open-weights / decentralized AI | Strong positive |
| Prior public writing supporting closed-model / concentrated-compute AI theses | Neutral; not a disqualifier but the candidate must be able to articulate the case for PRSM's architecture before confirmation |
| Record of public governance service (IETF / W3C / academic peer-review / etc.) | Strong positive |
| Prior crypto foundation board service | Positive; prior governance experience matters |
| Founder-archetype operator with no governance background | Neutral; some seats benefit from operational bias |

## 4. Per-seat job descriptions

### 4.1 Academic / research seat (1-2 seats)

**Mission.** Bring frontier-AI research rigor, publication-standard
thinking, and academic-institution governance experience to
Foundation decisions. Particularly: CIS-1 silicon-standard review,
R-track research-partnership approvals, peer-reviewed security
methodology.

**Time commitment.** ~15 hrs/month average; 2 in-person board
meetings/year (~3 days each); 1 public event representation/year
expected.

**Required experience.**
- PhD + faculty-track or staff-scientist role at a frontier-AI lab
  or top-tier CS research department (MIT CSAIL / Berkeley BAIR /
  CMU / Stanford HAI / Allen Institute / NIST / similar).
- Publication record in at least one of: cryptography, distributed
  systems, ML security, confidential compute, governance.
- ≥5 years post-PhD; no upper bound.
- Demonstrated governance service: editorial board, program chair,
  institutional review board, or similar.

**Preferred experience.**
- Prior foundation board service in the crypto / open-source space.
- Public writing on decentralized-AI or open-weights policy.
- Ability to represent PRSM at academic venues (NeurIPS, USENIX,
  IEEE S&P) as an independent voice.

**Reports to.** Full Foundation board; bound by charter § 4.4
governance rules.

**Key decisions this seat leads on.**
- CIS-1 Technical Advisory Committee coordination (TAC convenes
  around certification-level amendments and interprets ambiguities
  in the normative spec).
- R-track research partnerships — is the proposed MPC / FHE / PQ
  cryptography partnership worth the Foundation's time?
- Any scientific-integrity question: publication protocols for
  audit reports, disclosure policy for discovered vulnerabilities,
  etc.

**Compensation.** Annual stipend equivalent ~$50K USD, paid in FTNS
(quarterly, at the FTNS-USD reference price on payment date). Travel
+ reasonable expenses reimbursed at cost.

### 4.2 Independent technical seat (1-2 seats)

**Mission.** Provide deep cryptographic / security engineering review
independent of any PRSM-implementer business relationship. This is the
"the auditor's auditor" seat — not a reviewer of protocol specs but a
check on whether the Foundation is making credible security decisions.

**Time commitment.** ~15 hrs/month average; 2 in-person meetings/year;
sporadic "I need a second opinion" on-calls as security questions arise.

**Required experience.**
- ≥10 years security/cryptography engineering in at least one of:
  hardware security (TEE, secure enclaves), applied cryptography,
  protocol design, smart-contract security.
- Demonstrated track record of identifying critical vulnerabilities
  in protocol code (CVE authorship / audit reports / public
  disclosures) OR credible equivalent (formal-verification work,
  zero-knowledge circuit review, etc.).
- **No commercial relationship with any PRSM implementer** (see §3.1).
  This is the hardest-to-fill criterion — the ideal candidate is
  an independent consultant or an academic with security practice.

**Preferred experience.**
- Prior board service at a foundation or security standards body.
- Experience reviewing specifications for neutrality / adversarial
  resistance (IETF / ISO / NIST publication-level editorial).
- Comfort with public disagreement — this seat must be willing to
  vote "no" on a board decision with only a minority.

**Reports to.** Full Foundation board.

**Key decisions this seat leads on.**
- Emergency-response activation (security-severity assessment).
- Formal audit firm selection + scope approval.
- Critical-vulnerability disclosure timing.
- Any standards-ratification amendment flagged as "security-critical"
  per GOV-1 §9.

**Compensation.** Same as §4.1 — ~$50K USD-equivalent FTNS stipend.

### 4.3 PRSM-staked community seat (2 seats)

**Mission.** Represent the interests of FTNS stakers / protocol
operators in Foundation decisions. The "we paid our stake and we have
skin in the game" voice.

**Time commitment.** ~10 hrs/month average; 2 in-person meetings/year.
Expected to maintain public presence (forum, Discord, published
decisions explained to the community).

**Required experience.**
- **Phase 7+:** elected by token-weighted staker vote (must hold
  minimum stake per then-current governance rules).
- **Pre-Phase 7 / bootstrap:** appointed by founder, confirmed by
  existing board. Must be a known protocol operator or early
  contributor with public track record.

**Preferred experience.**
- Running a T3 or T4 PRSM node or equivalent operator role in a
  comparable protocol (running an Ethereum validator, a Filecoin
  storage provider, etc.).
- Comfort with public community representation.
- No disqualifying conflict under §3.1 — this seat is notably
  NOT filled by paid Prismatica staff.

**Key decisions this seat leads on.**
- Protocol-parameter amendments affecting stakers (halving schedule,
  stake-slash rates).
- Economic-model white-paper review.
- Supply-diversity standard governance (PRSM-SUPPLY-1).

**Compensation.** Annual stipend ~$35K USD-equivalent FTNS (slightly
below technical/academic seats reflecting the lower expertise bar).
Travel reimbursed.

### 4.4 Ecosystem operators seat (1 seat, rotating)

**Mission.** Bring operator-level "the rubber meets the road" insight
to Foundation decisions. This seat rotates between representative
types (meganode operator, chip designer, model publisher) to avoid
any single operator archetype dominating.

**Time commitment.** ~10 hrs/month; 2 in-person meetings/year.
Expected to bring operator-reality context to policy decisions.

**Required experience.**
- Currently operating in the PRSM ecosystem in a material role
  (not just a participant).
- Examples of qualifying experience:
  - Runs one or more T4 meganodes or operates a T3 cloud-arbitrage
    fleet with ≥10 nodes;
  - Principal hardware engineer at a chip-design firm (pre-silicon
    or post-silicon implementer) that has either shipped PRSM-
    compatible hardware or has a credible roadmap;
  - Published-weights model-author who releases on the PRSM
    marketplace and has a sustained presence.
- Credibility check: minimum 6 months as an active ecosystem operator.

**Preferred experience.**
- Public-voice presence (technical blog / conference speaking).
- Prior operator-representative role in any protocol governance.

**Rotation mechanism.** Seat rotates every 2 years (not the standard
3-year term) to keep operator-type diversity. Sub-categorized terms:
- Year 1-2: meganode operator
- Year 3-4: chip designer
- Year 5-6: model publisher
- Repeat cycle.

**Compensation.** Annual stipend ~$35K USD-equivalent FTNS; travel.

### 4.5 Founder seat (bootstrap only — by GOV-1 §4.4 + §8)

**Mission.** During the bootstrap phase only, the founder holds one
board seat to ensure continuity between protocol engineering and
Foundation governance.

**Expiration.** This seat dissolves at the end of the bootstrap phase
per GOV-1 §8.3 — when the founder must choose between Foundation
board service (uncompensated) and Prismatica executive role (with
Prismatica compensation). PRSM-GOV-1 §8.1 defines bootstrap phase as
ending no later than 36 months after Foundation formation.

**Compensation.** **Uncompensated** per GOV-1 §8.2.

**Authority limits during bootstrap** per GOV-1 §8.2:
- Founder must recuse on any decision involving Prismatica
  (contracts, certifications, revenue flows).
- Founder may not vote on their own seat renewal / compensation.
- Founder must disclose all material conflicts prospectively.

## 5. Executive Director JD

### 5.1 Mission

Day-to-day operational lead of the Foundation. Reports to the full
board; executes board-approved strategy; represents the Foundation
externally; manages Foundation staff.

### 5.2 Required experience

- 10-15 years leadership experience, at least 5 at a protocol / open-
  source / standards-body / non-profit-tech ED or senior-leadership level.
- Budget execution at $5-20M scale with public financial reporting
  experience.
- Crypto-native or deeply crypto-fluent — able to represent FTNS
  denominated operations at face value to banking, audit, regulatory
  counterparties.
- **Zero material equity or executive role at Prismatica or any other
  PRSM implementer** (GOV-1 §4.5). Non-negotiable.

### 5.3 Key responsibilities

- Board prep (materials, minutes, quarterly updates).
- Staff management (currently TBD; projected first-year: technical
  writer, community manager, finance officer + part-time counsel).
- External representation (regulatory, media, partnerships).
- Grant-program execution within board-approved envelope (grants
  >$50K require board vote per GOV-1 §4.5).
- Quarterly transparency report publication.

### 5.4 Compensation

Market rate for non-profit / protocol-foundation ED. Public
benchmarks:
- Ethereum Foundation ED roles: $300-500K USD range (equivalent).
- Web3 Foundation senior leadership: ~$250-400K USD range.
- US 501(c)(3) EDs at similar asset scale: $180-300K USD range.

**Target range:** $250-400K USD-equivalent, paid partly in USDC + partly
in FTNS (board-approved split, reviewed annually). Standard
severance (6 mo at-will). No equity, no bonus tied to protocol
price. Travel reimbursed at cost.

### 5.5 Authority + limits (GOV-1 §4.5)

ED has **no vote** on:
- Standards ratification,
- Certification decisions,
- Contract upgrades,
- Grants >$50K.

ED may NOT hold material equity or executive role in Prismatica or
any implementer — an irrevocable constitutional limit.

## 8. Preconditions before outreach

Do not send the §6 emails before:

- [ ] Legal counsel engagement completed (jurisdiction-specific
  counsel confirms the board structure works in the Cayman
  Foundation Company context).
- [ ] Foundation entity actually formed (not just committed to) — a
  candidate asked "who is signing my letter of appointment?" should
  have a real answer.
- [ ] Multi-sig provisioned so the Foundation has on-chain authority
  over its treasury (otherwise compensation in FTNS is a promise
  without delivery infrastructure).
- [ ] Compensation philosophy §2 ratified by whatever body currently
  has that authority (founder + co-founder informally, then the
  first-confirmed board votes it into formal policy).
- [ ] Independence conflict-check rubric §3 reviewed with legal
  counsel for any jurisdiction-specific adjustments.
- [ ] Founder has identified candidates through their own network and
  has permission to cold-outreach (vs needing a mutual-contact intro).

## 9. What this doc does NOT do

- **Does not commit PRSM to any candidate.** All selection is board-
  reserved per GOV-1 §4.4.
- **Does not substitute for legal counsel review.** Jurisdiction-
  specific obligations (residency requirements, disclosure rules,
  beneficial-owner filings) may require JD revisions.
- **Does not set final compensation.** The §2 magnitudes are
  recommended starting points; the Foundation's finance committee
  sets actual numbers once formed.
- **Does not address ED search logistics.** The §5 + §6.4 sections
  scope the role but running the search is a separate recruiter
  engagement, not this doc.
