## I. Acknowledgment of intent

PRSM Foundation Ltd. (the "Foundation") acknowledges receipt of notice from Prismatica AI Corp. (the "Company") of the Company's intent, pursuant to the Company's `PRISMATICA-UWC-2026-05-XX-1` Unanimous Written Consent dated _______________, to contribute Five Hundred Thousand U.S. Dollars ($500,000) denominated in Circle USDC to the Foundation for the express and sole purpose of seeding the Aerodrome USDC-FTNS liquidity pool on Base mainnet pursuant to `PRSM-CR-2026-05-13-1` (the "Pool-Seed Ceremony").

The Foundation has separately ratified `PRSM-CR-2026-05-13-1` authorizing the Pool-Seed Ceremony, conditioned upon the conditions set forth in §5 thereof, one of which is the receipt and Basescan-verification of the Company's contribution.

## II. Confirmation of intended use

The Foundation confirms that, upon receipt of the contribution, the funds will be:

1. Held in the Foundation's multi-signature treasury wallet ("Foundation Safe") at on-chain address `0x91b0e6F85A371D82De94eD13A3812d9f5A4E5791` on Base mainnet (chain ID 8453);

2. Deployed exclusively to the Pool-Seed Ceremony described in `PRSM-CR-2026-05-13-1` §3.2, namely: approval to and provision of liquidity into the Aerodrome USDC-FTNS volatile pool via the Aerodrome Router contract at `0xcF77a3Ba9A5CA399B7c97c74d54e5b1Beb874E43`, with `500,000 USDC` + `2,000,000 FTNS` (the latter sourced separately from the Foundation's existing treasury) at an implied bootstrap price of $0.25 per FTNS;

3. Not deployed to: (a) Foundation general operating expenses unrelated to the Pool-Seed Ceremony; (b) any third party other than the Aerodrome Router contract in the course of the seed transaction; (c) speculative trading or yield-farming activities; (d) any purpose inconsistent with the Foundation's Cayman Islands non-profit governance charter.

## III. Conditional use-or-return provision

The Foundation undertakes that if, for any reason, the Pool-Seed Ceremony has not been executed within sixty (60) days of receipt of the contribution, or if the Pool-Seed Ceremony is formally cancelled or superseded by Foundation council resolution prior to execution, the Foundation shall return the unspent portion of the contribution to the Company's source wallet (or such other on-chain address as the Company designates in writing) within an additional thirty (30) days.

For the avoidance of doubt: upon successful execution of the Pool-Seed Ceremony, the contribution is fully deployed and no return obligation remains.

## IV. Characterization

The Foundation confirms its understanding that the contribution shall be characterized, in both parties' books and records, as follows:

1. **Not a security.** The contribution is not an investment of money in a common enterprise with an expectation of profits derived from the efforts of others within the meaning of *SEC v. W.J. Howey Co.* The Foundation issues no equity, debt, profit-share, governance right, token, or other reciprocal contractual performance obligation to the Company in exchange for or in connection with the contribution. The resulting liquidity-provider token position from the Pool-Seed Ceremony is held by the Foundation, not the Company, and the Company has no claim against such position or any fee income generated thereby.

2. **Not a charitable donation requiring tax-deductibility treatment.** The Foundation makes no representation that the contribution qualifies for a U.S. charitable-contribution deduction in the hands of the Company. Tax characterization in the Company's books is a matter for the Company's tax advisors.

3. **Not a quid-pro-quo exchange for services.** The Foundation undertakes no service, performance, or deliverable obligation to the Company in connection with the contribution. The Foundation's execution of the Pool-Seed Ceremony is undertaken pursuant to its own governance authorization (`PRSM-CR-2026-05-13-1`), not as performance owed to the Company.

4. **Ecosystem-development expense in the Company's hands.** The Foundation acknowledges, without warranting the tax-law characterization in the Company's books, that the Company's characterization of the contribution as an "ecosystem development expense" (per `PRISMATICA-UWC-2026-05-XX-1` §III Resolution 3) is consistent with the substance of the transaction and the architectural rationale set forth in `PRSM-CR-2026-05-13-1` §2.3.

## Pairing notes

**Two-document architecture:** The Prismatica Written Consent authorizes the wire from the Company's side; this Letter of Expected Receipt acknowledges intent + commits Foundation to the conditional use-or-return from the Foundation's side. Together they form the contemporaneous documentary record of the transaction.

**Why issue this letter BEFORE the wire executes:**

1. Locks in the agreed characterization (§IV) before funds change hands — neither party can later renegotiate by claiming surprise on terms.
2. Gives the Company contemporaneous documentation supporting its `PRISMATICA-UWC-2026-05-XX-1` §III Resolution 3 ecosystem-development-expense characterization.
3. Triggers the Foundation's use-or-return undertaking (§III) at receipt, not at some later confirmation step — protects Prismatica against the edge case where the ceremony is delayed or cancelled after wire receipt.

**Why this letter is one-way (Foundation → Prismatica) rather than a bilateral contract:**

A bilateral contract that bound both parties to reciprocal performance could itself convert the contribution into a securities transaction (Howey's "common enterprise + expectation of profits from the efforts of others"). The one-way letter posture — Foundation unilaterally acknowledges receipt + commits to use-or-return + commits to non-undertakings — preserves the no-securities characterization while still locking in the agreed terms. Each party's authorization is its own governance instrument (Foundation: PRSM-CR-2026-05-13-1; Prismatica: PRISMATICA-UWC-2026-05-XX-1).

**This letter is paired with Prismatica's Written Consent + the on-chain wire, NOT replaced by them.** Treat all three as contemporaneous artifacts forming a single transaction record.
