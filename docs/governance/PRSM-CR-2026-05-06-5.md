## 2. Background

### 2.1 The unaffordable-audit-stack problem

`audits/AUDIT_PLAN.md` v1.1 and `audits/rfp/README.md` document an 8-RFP audit pipeline (L3 + L4-firm + L4-Code4rena + L5 + L6f + L7 + L8 + L9) with a combined budget envelope of **$280,000–$645,000**. All 8 RFP packets were drafted by 2026-05-05 and are sitting awaiting founder execution of vendor outreach.

PRSM is currently a sole-founder, open-source, pre-funding project. None of the RFP budget is available. The implicit project policy — "external audits clear before mainnet deploys of audit-bundle / Phase 8 / Phase 7-storage / PublisherKeyAnchor" — is, under current resource constraints, a project-freeze policy. Tasks #31 ("Phase 7 Task 9 — External audit + mainnet deploy, hardware-gated") and #40 ("Phase 7.1 Task 9 — Audit + deploy, bundled, hardware-gated") have been "in_progress" since 2026-04 with no realistic path to completion under the implicit policy.

### 2.2 What's worked internally

The L2 internal AI multi-team review (tasks #325–#327) closed **1 CRITICAL + 7 HIGH** findings via the `agent-teams` skill — parallel, independent reviews from multiple AI agent personas — and produced a consolidated findings document at `audits/findings/consolidated.md`. All findings were remediated (tasks #346–#352). This has demonstrably worked as a coverage layer.

The agent-teams modality is a good-faith effort to surface obvious issues. It is not equivalent to a paid Trail of Bits review. But it is **substantially better than zero external review**, and it is *something the Foundation can actually do today* without funding.

### 2.3 The L8 Track B carve-out

US securities counsel for any future Reg D 506(c) Prismatica solicitation is materially different from the other deferred layers. Soliciting accredited investors without counsel review is a personal-liability legal exposure for the founder, not just a project-risk item. POL-2 retains L8 Track B as a hard gate triggered by the first investor solicitation, regardless of the broader audit-budget situation.

### 2.4 Context — today's mainnet posture

As of this resolution:

- FTNS token + Foundation Safe (sole admin, post-CR-3) are in production use on Base mainnet.
- ProvenanceRegistry v1 + v2 are deployed, verified, and have demonstrated end-to-end royalty distribution (tx `0xbe85b53f...4fe5b20`).
- The Foundation Safe has earned its first 0.2 FTNS in real network fees.
- These deployments did not have any L3/L4/L5/L7/L8/L9 audit clearance. The project shipped because the contracts in scope were structurally simpler — no fund custody, no slashing, no economic-game-theory — and reviewable by AI multi-team alone.
- The remaining undeployed contracts (audit-bundle, Phase 8 emission, Phase 7-storage, PublisherKeyAnchor) are structurally more complex (custody, slashing, role admin) and were originally gated by the implicit policy.

This resolution closes that gap.

## 3. Resolved

The council hereby resolves:

**RESOLVED 1.** The council adopts **PRSM-POL-2 (Resource-Constrained Audit Strategy)**, version 1.0, as published in `docs/governance/2026-05-06-resource-constrained-audit-strategy.md`. POL-2 is incorporated into this resolution by reference.

**RESOLVED 2.** The implicit "all external audits clear before mainnet deploy" policy is **superseded** for the layers POL-2 covers (L3, L4-firm, L4-Code4rena, L5, L6f, L7, L8 Track A, L9). For each, the new gate is the substitute mitigation specified in POL-2 §4.

**RESOLVED 3.** **L8 Track B (US securities counsel) remains a hard gate**, triggered by the first Prismatica investor solicitation, regardless of the broader budget situation. POL-2 §3 and §5 govern.

**RESOLVED 4.** `audits/rfp/README.md` shall be retagged: each deferred layer's "Status" column updated from "RFP drafted" to "RFP drafted — DEFERRED per PRSM-POL-2 (funding-gated)". The README's outreach-priority section shall be revised to reflect the POL-2 trigger framework rather than presenting the RFPs as actionable today. Individual RFP packet files remain in tree as audit-trail evidence — they will be ready to fire when revisit triggers fire.

**RESOLVED 5.** Tasks #31 ("Phase 7 Task 9 — External audit + mainnet deploy") and #40 ("Phase 7.1 Task 9 — Audit + deploy, bundled") are **resolved by this policy**, not by completing the originally-described external-audit work. New criteria for actual audit-bundle mainnet deploy: agent-teams self-audit complete + OZ Pausable verified + TVL cap defined + 14-day public review window passed (per POL-2 §4). When those criteria are met, deploy may proceed.

**RESOLVED 6.** The agent-teams self-audit modality is **ratified as the substitute external-audit modality** for L3 and L4 pre-mainnet review under POL-2 §4.1. Its limitations are explicitly acknowledged (POL-2 §6) and its findings shall be filed at `audits/findings/L<N>-self-<date>/` with the same disclosure hygiene as paid audits.

**RESOLVED 7.** The §5 revisit-triggers schedule in POL-2 binds the council. Annual review on issuance anniversary (next: 2027-05-06). Trigger-event review immediate upon §5 events.

**RESOLVED 8.** The L9 bug-bounty gate is flipped from "post-L4" to "active now via security@prsm.network" per POL-2 §3. The `SECURITY.md` disclosure policy (already published 2026-05-05 per L10d task #342) is the operative incoming-report channel.

## 5. Explicit non-scope

This resolution does NOT:

- Change the FTNS economic parameters (`MAX_SUPPLY`, `NETWORK_FEE_BPS`, the 10/2/88 split, or the halving schedule).
- Modify the Foundation Safe's signer set or signing threshold.
- Authorize any minting beyond `INITIAL_SUPPLY = 100M` (already issued).
- Authorize any treasury disbursement (governed by PRSM-POL-1).
- Modify the FTNS role-admin state established in PRSM-CR-2026-05-06-3.
- Authorize the actual mainnet deploy of any specific contract — POL-2 §4 defines a *process* for those deploys; each individual deploy still requires the founder to execute the agent-teams self-audit + Pausable check + TVL-cap definition + 14-day window for that specific contract.
- Waive the L8 Track B gate. (RESOLVED 3 explicitly retains it.)
- Permanently retire any of the originally-planned external audits — they are deferred, not cancelled. POL-2 §5 lists explicit revisit triggers.
