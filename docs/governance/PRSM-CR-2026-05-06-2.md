# PRSM Council Resolution PRSM-CR-2026-05-06-2

**Resolution ID:** PRSM-CR-2026-05-06-2
**Type:** Foundation Council Resolution — sole-founder ratification
**Issued:** 2026-05-06
**Subject:** Authorization to deploy `ProvenanceRegistryV2.sol` to
Base mainnet on existing review evidence, without engaging an
external L4 smart-contract audit firm specifically for this contract.

---

## 1. Authority

The PRSM Foundation council, currently composed of a single member
(the founder, pending expansion to 2-of-3 per the governance charter),
adopts this resolution under the authority granted by:

- `docs/2026-04-21-prsm-gov-1-foundation-governance-charter.md` —
  Foundation governance charter, council role + voting rules.
- `audits/AUDIT_PLAN.md` §5 (Per-layer detail), which establishes the
  L4 external smart-contract audit layer and identifies the council
  as the body responsible for relaxing audit gates on a per-contract
  basis when justified by contract scope and risk profile.
- `docs/governance/2026-05-05-treasury-reserve-and-risk-transfer-policy.md`
  (PRSM-POL-1) §5 (Disbursement-authorization tiers), which governs
  how treasury funds are committed but does NOT itself gate
  non-fund-custodying contract deployments. This resolution clarifies
  that the L4 gate on `ProvenanceRegistryV2` is a separate question
  from the treasury-disbursement framework and is properly resolved
  here.

At the present council size (1), council unanimity (1-of-1) is the
operative quorum, identical to the standard applied in
`PRSM-CR-2026-05-06-1`.

## 2. Background

`ProvenanceRegistryV2.sol` was shipped on 2026-05-06 (commit
`19998a33`) and deployed to Base Sepolia on the same day at
`0xe75F0c24a9e63B63456d170d99F03Ab7fC3450A7`. The contract's purpose
is to record per-content embedding commitments — a 32-byte
`keccak256(model_id || uint32_be(dim) || vector_bytes)` value plus an
optional fingerprint-kind tag — alongside the existing creator,
royalty rate, and metadata URI fields carried over from
`ProvenanceRegistry` v1.

The deploy script (`contracts/scripts/deploy-provenance-registry-v2.js`,
introduced 2026-05-06) hard-blocks `--network base` / `--network mainnet`
deploys with an explicit reference to PRSM-PROV-1 plan §4.5 + this
audit gate. That guard is appropriate as a default; this resolution
records the conditions under which it may be removed for
`ProvenanceRegistryV2` specifically, by a follow-up commit deleting
the guard, in a separate reviewable PR.

The factual record supporting this resolution:

- **Hardhat tests:** 15 tests covering positive registration,
  duplicate-content rejection, royalty-rate cap, embedding-commitment
  binding, anti-zero-forgery guard, ownership transfer, and storage
  layout invariants. Tests committed in the same change as the
  contract (commit `19998a33`).
- **Python unit tests:** 33 tests covering
  `ProvenanceRegistryV2Client`, `compute_embedding_commitment`
  (canonical formula), `compute_kind_tag`, `dispute_provenance`
  round-trip including a Hardhat-agreement cross-check against the
  Solidity-side `keccak256` computation.
- **Live Sepolia exercise (today):** Three on-chain transactions
  successfully verified all three load-bearing safety properties
  against the deployed Base Sepolia address:
  - Positive dispute round-trip: same-vector dispute returns true
    (tx `0x913e2d9deb8cc98f457e290bce0111a79fdbe8ffce4f94269852c9a2ee287a59`).
  - Negative dispute (substituted vector): different-vector dispute
    returns false. Defense against cross-model spoofing.
  - Anti-zero-forgery: `verifyEmbeddingCommitment(_, zero)` returns
    false even when the on-chain commitment is also zero. Defense
    against squatter attacks against legacy byte-hash-only content
    (tx `0xc31d11998f6fc753b1cc82db43eb4824da2e31fee2f100bc380e218d255d6a5e`).
- **Threat model documentation:**
  `docs/2026-05-06-prsm-prov-1-threat-model-addendum-item-7.md`
  §3.18 enumerates six adversaries against the V2 surface (A1–A6:
  cross-model spoofing, truncation/extension, squatter attacks,
  zero-commitment forgery, storage-layout corruption, vector
  exposure on dispute) and documents the mitigation for each.
- **Codex review pattern:** PRSM contracts have historically been
  reviewed with the seven-round Codex independent code review
  process described in `audits/AUDIT_PLAN.md` §5 (L0). Equivalent
  rounds for `ProvenanceRegistryV2` are tracked under the
  PRSM-PROV-1 Item 7 progress log.

## 3. Resolved

**RESOLVED**, that the council finds the existing review evidence
specified in §2 of this resolution **sufficient** to authorize
deployment of `ProvenanceRegistryV2.sol` to Base mainnet, and that
external L4 smart-contract audit-firm engagement is **not required**
for this specific contract before mainnet deploy.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that this finding is bound to the version of
`contracts/contracts/ProvenanceRegistryV2.sol` at the same git commit
as this resolution, identified by the commit hash that contains both
this file and the contract source. Any subsequent material change to
the contract source — defined as any change other than (a)
re-formatting that preserves bytecode, (b) comment-only changes, or
(c) a verifier-only change to event-emission ordering that does not
alter storage layout — voids this authorization and reinstates the
L4 gate.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the cost-benefit reasoning supporting this
finding is hereby recorded for future reviewers:

1. `ProvenanceRegistryV2` is a **pure storage contract**. It records
   embedding commitments and content metadata. It does NOT custody
   FTNS, does NOT route fund flows, does NOT have an admin role, and
   does NOT have any upgrade hooks. The maximum economic harm a bug
   in this contract could cause is **incorrect dispute resolution
   evidence** for content registered under v2 — not theft of
   royalties, not drain of treasury, not loss of staked FTNS.
2. The royalty distribution path that DOES custody and route funds
   continues to flow through `RoyaltyDistributor.sol` and
   `ProvenanceRegistry` v1, both of which were audited under prior
   gates and continue to operate under the existing L4 cover.
3. The contract is **non-upgradeable** — there is no admin role and
   no upgrade hooks, so a discovered bug post-deploy can be addressed
   only by deploying v3 alongside (the same pattern by which v2 sits
   alongside v1 today). That migration path is exercised end-to-end
   on Sepolia as of today and is documented in
   `docs/2026-05-06-content-provenance-correctness-plan.md`.
4. The full economic envelope of an external L4 firm engagement for
   this single non-fund-custodying contract is per
   `audits/AUDIT_PLAN.md` §4 estimated at $20K–$80K and 2–6 weeks of
   calendar time. That spend does not pencil against the contract's
   risk profile.
5. The cumulative review evidence in §2 (Hardhat + Python + live
   Sepolia + threat model + codex pattern) is the same evidence
   stack that informed prior council-ratified contract deploys
   under L0/L2 review when those deploys did not custody funds.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that this authorization is **scoped** strictly
to `ProvenanceRegistryV2.sol`. The L4 audit gate on the broader
audit-bundle stack — specifically the fund-custodying contracts in
the Phase 7 storage and Phase 7.1 consensus bundles, including
`StorageSlashing.sol`, `EscrowPool.sol`, `StakeBond.sol`,
`KeyDistribution.sol`, `EmissionController.sol`,
`CompensationDistributor.sol`, and any upgradeable variants thereof,
plus the `Ed25519Library.sol` chain-side cryptographic verifier
addressed by L3 — **remains in force**. Tasks #31 (Phase 7 Task 9 —
external audit + mainnet deploy) and #40 (Phase 7.1 Task 9 — audit +
deploy) continue to be hardware-gated AND audit-firm-gated.

## 4. Conditions on the authorization

The authorization granted in §3 is conditioned on the following
operational requirements being satisfied at deploy time:

1. **Pre-deploy verification.** The deploy ceremony for the mainnet
   `ProvenanceRegistryV2` shall use the same hardware-multisig
   signing infrastructure documented in
   `~/.prsm-foundation-private/Multi-Sig_Addresses.txt` for any
   contract-creation transaction issued to Base mainnet.
2. **Address publication.** The mainnet contract address shall be
   added to `prsm/deployments/contract_addresses.json` under the
   `base` entry in the same commit that removes the deploy-script
   `mainnet` guard, so the address-of-record and the gate-removal
   are atomically reviewable.
3. **Post-deploy invariant verification.** The deploy script's
   post-deploy check sequence (MAX_ROYALTY_RATE_BPS == 9800,
   `contents(zero).creator == ZeroAddress`, and
   `verifyEmbeddingCommitment(zero, zero) == false`) must pass
   against the deployed mainnet address before the address is
   added to the deployments file. The retry-on-empty pattern that
   defends against cross-replica RPC propagation lag (introduced
   in commit `721174ed`) applies.
4. **Live exercise on mainnet.** The
   `scripts/exercise_provenance_registry_v2_sepolia.py` script
   (which despite its name is network-agnostic via env var) shall
   be run against the mainnet deployment with a small-stakes
   register-and-dispute round trip before the contract is
   advertised to producers via documentation. The Sepolia evidence
   already covers protocol correctness; the mainnet exercise covers
   chain-specific factors (gas behavior, finality, RPC behavior on
   the production endpoint).
5. **Public disclosure.** The mainnet deployment shall be disclosed
   in the next quarterly transparency report per PRSM-POL-1 §7.1,
   including the deploy tx hash, deployer address, and a pointer to
   the Basescan verification page for the deployed bytecode.
6. **Bug-bounty backstop.** Concurrent with the mainnet deploy, the
   `ProvenanceRegistryV2` contract address shall be added to the
   scope of the Immunefi bug bounty program designed under L9 (see
   `audits/AUDIT_PLAN.md` §5 L9 entry, gated on L4 first-pass
   completion for *fund-custodying* contracts; the V2 storage
   contract's inclusion does not depend on that prerequisite). The
   bounty payout for V2 is set at the standard non-fund-custodying
   tier per the L9 program design.

If any of conditions 1–4 fail at deploy time, the deploy is aborted
and the L4 gate reinstates automatically without further council
action.

## 5. What this resolution does NOT do

To prevent scope creep, the following are explicitly outside the
scope of this resolution:

1. **It does not relax L4 for the Phase 7 storage bundle.** Tasks
   #31 / #40 remain audit-firm-gated. The fund-custodying contracts
   in those bundles are categorically distinct from
   `ProvenanceRegistryV2` and require external firm engagement
   before mainnet deploy.
2. **It does not relax L3** (cryptographic spec-level review for
   `Ed25519Library.sol` and any successor crypto primitive that
   touches consensus). L3 has its own gate documented in task #344
   and is independent of L4.
3. **It does not authorize foundation-treasury disbursements** to
   pay deploy gas. The gas for the V2 mainnet deploy comes from the
   existing testnet-deployer wallet at
   `0xBbEB1cb42F1D5ad05B46eE023D6e4871D813C9a0` (already documented
   for the Sepolia deploy) or from a pre-funded operational wallet
   sized within PRSM-POL-1 §5 tier-1 thresholds (< $10K, founder +
   1 signer authorization), as appropriate for the small gas spend
   on a non-fund-custodying contract creation.
4. **It does not commit to any specific deploy date.** Mainnet
   deployment proceeds when operational readiness (hardware-multisig
   ceremony scheduling, monitoring infrastructure, V2Client
   end-to-end exercise on Sepolia maintained green for at least one
   additional week) supports it.
5. **It does not amend `audits/AUDIT_PLAN.md`** to remove the L4
   row. The audit plan continues to identify L4 as a per-major-
   release gate; this resolution adopts a per-contract exception for
   `ProvenanceRegistryV2` based on the cost-benefit analysis in §3
   #1–#5, not a general policy that removes L4 across the audit-
   bundle stack.
