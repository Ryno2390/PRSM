## 2. Background

### 2.1 Current state (verified on-chain 2026-05-06)

The FTNS token (`FTNSTokenSimple` at `0x5276a3756C85f2E9e46f6D34386167a209aa16e5` on Base mainnet, chain 8453) defines four AccessControl roles:

| Role | Selector | Authority |
|------|----------|-----------|
| `DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE` | `0x00…00` | Grant/revoke any role |
| `MINTER_ROLE` | `keccak256("MINTER_ROLE")` | Mint up to `MAX_SUPPLY = 1,000,000,000 × 10^18` |
| `PAUSER_ROLE` | `keccak256("PAUSER_ROLE")` | Pause/unpause transfers |
| `BURNER_ROLE` | `keccak256("BURNER_ROLE")` | Burn from any address |

All four roles are presently held by a single hot-key EOA (`0x8eaA00FF741323bc8B0ab1290c544738D9b2f012`), the original FTNS deployer. None are held by the Foundation Safe (`0x91b0e6F85A371D82De94eD13A3812d9f5A4E5791`).

### 2.2 Why this is urgent

`MAX_SUPPLY = 1B FTNS` and `INITIAL_SUPPLY = 100M FTNS`. The hot key with MINTER_ROLE could currently mint up to **900,000,000 additional FTNS** — a 10× supply expansion — without any Foundation Safe involvement. The 2026-05-06 FTNS supply migration (tx `0xa128e826…d942`) closed the *custody* gap (treasury holdings now in Safe), but did not address the *issuance* gap (MINTER_ROLE still on the hot key).

PAUSER_ROLE and BURNER_ROLE on a hot key are smaller risks but still warrant migration: a compromised key could pause all FTNS transfers (denial of service) or burn from any account (with BURNER_ROLE being the more severe of the two).

### 2.3 Why now and not earlier

The hot-key state was originally documented as transitional in the 2026-04-30 verification record (memory `project_production_ftns_state_2026_04_30.md`): *"DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE is held by a hot wallet whose seed will be loaded onto one of the hardware multi-sig devices arriving 2026-05-01."* That migration plan was deferred when the multi-sig hardware arrived (Foundation Safe was set up but admin handoff was not part of the initial commissioning ceremony). The 2026-05-06 mainnet bring-up Path A trace (`docs/2026-05-06-canonical-workflow-base-mainnet-trace.md`) explicitly listed admin role migration as a deferred follow-up.

This resolution closes that deferred item.

### 2.4 Why this is a one-way operation

`renounceRole` on `DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE` is intentionally disabled on FTNSTokenSimple (line 147: *"DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE renounce disabled - use grantRole(new) + revokeRole(old)"*). Migration must therefore use `grantRole` followed by `revokeRole`. Once `revokeRole(DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE, hot_key)` is executed and confirmed, the hot key permanently loses admin authority. There is no rollback. If the Foundation Safe loses access (e.g., 2-of-3 quorum becomes unreachable), and the hot key has been revoked, the FTNS contract becomes administratively orphaned. Future role grants/revokes become impossible.

This risk is mitigated — but not eliminated — by:

- Foundation Safe's 2-of-3 hardware design (any single signer loss does not break the quorum).
- Off-chain backup of all 3 hardware-wallet seed phrases per L6c key-rotation runbook.
- The fact that an orphaned FTNS contract still functions for token transfers; only role administration is broken.

The council judges this risk acceptable in light of the larger risk of leaving MINTER_ROLE on a hot key indefinitely.

## 3. Resolved

The council hereby resolves:

**RESOLVED 1.** The four AccessControl roles on FTNSTokenSimple at `0x5276a3756C85f2E9e46f6D34386167a209aa16e5` shall be migrated from the hot-key EOA `0x8eaA00FF741323bc8B0ab1290c544738D9b2f012` to the Foundation Safe `0x91b0e6F85A371D82De94eD13A3812d9f5A4E5791`. The four roles in scope:

  - `DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE`
  - `MINTER_ROLE`
  - `PAUSER_ROLE`
  - `BURNER_ROLE`

**RESOLVED 2.** The migration shall proceed in two phases as defined in §4 below. Phase 1 (grant) precedes Phase 2 (revoke). At no point shall the hot key revoke its own roles before the Foundation Safe has demonstrably been granted the corresponding role.

**RESOLVED 3.** Phase 2 (revoke) operations shall be initiated *from the Foundation Safe* (not from the hot key revoking itself). This ensures the Safe is exercised in production as the administrative authority, and that the revoke is end-to-end ratified by the 2-of-3 multisig quorum rather than dependent on the hot key's continued cooperation.

**RESOLVED 4.** After Phase 2 confirms on-chain for all four roles, the hot-key file (`~/.prsm/.env` `FTNS_WALLET_PRIVATE_KEY`) shall be zeroized per the L6c key-rotation runbook (§5 below). Specifically: backed up to encrypted offline storage, then deleted from the working laptop.

**RESOLVED 5.** The migration is authorized to proceed at the founder's operational convenience. The council notes that the residual MINTER_ROLE risk (§2.2) compounds with each passing day, and accordingly the founder is encouraged to schedule the ceremony **within the next 7 days**, subject to availability of all three hardware wallets (Ledger, Trezor, OneKey) and any travel constraints. This 7-day target is encouragement, not a binding deadline; if life intervenes, the council does not consider the resolution to be in violation, but the founder shall log the slippage and the reason in the §8 execution record.

## 4. Sequencing — phase-by-phase

### Phase 1 — hot key grants four roles to Safe (4 txs, hot-key signed)

For each role in `[DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE, MINTER_ROLE, PAUSER_ROLE, BURNER_ROLE]`, the hot key signs and broadcasts:

```solidity
FTNSTokenSimple(0x5276…16e5).grantRole(role, 0x91b0…5791)
```

Each tx must confirm with status=1 before proceeding to Phase 2. Verification: `hasRole(role, safe) == true` returns true via `eth_call` for all four roles.

**Cost:** ~4 × ~70k gas = ~280k gas total. At Base mainnet ~0.01 gwei = ~0.000003 ETH.

### Phase 2 — Foundation Safe revokes four roles from hot key (4 multisig ceremonies)

For each role, the founder proposes (Ledger), a hardware co-signer ratifies (Trezor or OneKey), and the tx executes:

```solidity
FTNSTokenSimple(0x5276…16e5).revokeRole(role, 0x8eaA…f012)
```

Order recommended: revoke MINTER_ROLE first (highest risk surface), then PAUSER_ROLE, then BURNER_ROLE, then DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE last. The DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE revoke is the truly one-way step; doing it last gives the Safe a chance to abort the migration cleanly if any of the prior revokes encounters an unexpected issue.

After each revoke confirms, verify on-chain: `hasRole(role, hot_key) == false`.

**Cost:** ~4 × ~70k gas (per revoke tx, plus a small Safe overhead). Two ceremonies per role × 4 roles = 8 ceremonies. Realistic time: ~30–45 minutes given the user has practiced the multisig flow on the 2026-05-06 trace.

### Phase 3 — Hot-key zeroization

After Phase 2 confirms for all four roles:

1. Verify on-chain: `hasRole(role, hot_key) == false` AND `hasRole(role, safe) == true` for all four roles.
2. Confirm in `prsm/deployments/contract_addresses.json` that the FTNS admin record reflects the new state.
3. Back up `~/.prsm/.env` `FTNS_WALLET_PRIVATE_KEY` to encrypted offline storage (e.g., printed to paper, sealed in a safe; or written to a hardware wallet dedicated to legacy-key custody; choice per L6c §3).
4. Delete the line from `~/.prsm/.env` and verify the file no longer contains the key.
5. Update `MEMORY.md` index to reflect the migrated state.

Phase 3 is administrative housekeeping, not on-chain. Once roles are revoked, the key is administratively useless on the FTNS contract — Phase 3 is about removing the hot-key file from the working laptop's filesystem so a laptop compromise can't exfiltrate the key (even though it has nothing on-chain to do with).

## 5. Conditions

This resolution is contingent on:

- The Foundation Safe being demonstrably operational (verified by the 2026-05-06 100-FTNS disbursement ceremony, audit log preserved).
- All three hardware wallets (Ledger, Trezor, OneKey) being available and known-functional at the time of Phase 2.
- The hot-key file `~/.prsm/.env` `FTNS_WALLET_PRIVATE_KEY` being available and known to derive to `0x8eaA00FF741323bc8B0ab1290c544738D9b2f012` at the time of Phase 1. Verification: derive the address from the key offline, compare bit-for-bit.

## 6. Explicit non-scope

This resolution does NOT:

- Address any role administration on contracts other than FTNSTokenSimple. (RoyaltyDistributor, ProvenanceRegistry v1/v2 do not have admin roles in the same sense; their `networkTreasury` arg is immutable for the RoyaltyDistributor.)
- Change `MAX_SUPPLY` (still 1B, unchanged) or `INITIAL_SUPPLY` (still 100M, already minted).
- Modify any economic parameters: `NETWORK_FEE_BPS` (200), `MAX_ROYALTY_RATE_BPS` (9800), or the canonical 10/2/88 split.
- Authorize any minting beyond the existing 100M (which would require a separate resolution naming the recipient, amount, and economic justification).
- Authorize any treasury disbursement (those follow PRSM-POL-1 §5 tiers, not this resolution).
- Govern the decision to migrate the RoyaltyDistributor settlement pattern from push to pull (see PRSM-CR-2026-05-06-4-DRAFT, separate document).
- Govern the decision to expand the council from 1 to 2-of-3 (separate governance-charter amendment, not in scope here).
- Authorize wholesale modification of `~/.prsm/.env`. Hot-key zeroization in Phase 3 (RESOLVED 4) specifically targets the `FTNS_WALLET_PRIVATE_KEY` line in that file; other entries (API keys for Anthropic / OpenAI / OpenRouter / HuggingFace, third-party credentials, and any other unrelated env vars) are out of scope and shall remain unchanged. The hot-key file's other contents are governed by their own respective rotation schedules and credential-management policies, not this resolution.
